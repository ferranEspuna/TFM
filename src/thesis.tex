\abstracteng{
Extremal problems for hypergraphs represent a central area of combinatorics, extending classical questions from graph theory.
A fundamental direction, initiated by Turán, concerns the maximum number of edges a $k$-uniform hypergraph can possess without containing a specific forbidden subgraph.
This thesis focuses on the degenerate Turán problem, where the forbidden subgraph $G$ is $k$-partite, resulting in a Turán density $\pi(G)=0$.
Specifically, we investigate the problem of finding complete balanced $k$-partite $k$-subgraphs, denoted $\compoverset{k}{t}$.

Classical results by Kővari-Sós-Turán (for $k=2$) and Erdős (for $k \ge 2$) provide upper bounds on the Turán number $\ex{n}{\compoverset{k}{t}}$,
showing it is $O(n^{k - \delta})$ for some $\delta > 0$ dependent on $t$ and $k$.
These bounds guarantee the existence of $\compoverset{k}{t}$ in any $k$-uniform hypergraph ($k$-graph) with sufficiently many edges.
However, the proofs are often non-constructive and do not yield efficient methods for locating such subgraphs.

This thesis presents a deterministic, polynomial-time algorithm that addresses this gap.
Given a $k$-graph $G$ with $n$ vertices and $m$ edges, the algorithm finds a complete balanced $k$-partite subgraph $\compoverset{k}{t}$.
The part size $t$ obtained depends explicitly on $n$, $k$, and the edge density parameter $d=m/n^k$,
closely matching the parameters implicit in the Erdős-type existence proofs (specifically, $t$ is approximately $(\log n / \log(1/d))^{1/(k-1)}$).

Our algorithm generalizes the $k=2$ case addressed by Mubayi and Turán, employing a recursive strategy based on analyzing appropriately defined link graphs.
The primary contribution is an efficient, constructive proof of the existence bounds for $\compoverset{k}{t}$ in dense hypergraphs,
demonstrating that these fundamental structures can not only be guaranteed to exist but can also be found algorithmically within polynomial time.
}
